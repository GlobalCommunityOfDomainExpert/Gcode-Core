# Colors

## Purpose

Colors define the visual identity of GCODE and create a consistent, trustworthy interface.

---

## Color Roles

### Primary

Used for structure.

Examples:
-

- Navigation
- Headers
- Active navigation
- Focus indicators
- Links

---

### Accent

Used for actions.

Examples:

- Primary buttons
- Call-to-action buttons
- Important actions

---

### Success

Used for positive outcomes.

Examples:

- Registration successful
- Completed tasks
- Certificates
- Success messages

---

### Warning

Used for caution.

Examples:

- Pending actions
- Deadlines
- Waitlists

---

### Danger

Used for destructive actions.

Examples:

- Delete
- Cancel
- Errors
- Failed operations

---

### Neutral

Neutral colors make up most of the interface.

Use them for:

- Backgrounds
- Cards
- Borders
- Dividers
- Text

---

## Design Principles

- Use color to reinforce meaning, not decoration.
- Keep the interface mostly neutral.
- Reserve Accent for important actions.
- Use semantic colors consistently across all pages.
- Never rely only on color to communicate information.
- Maintain accessible color contrast.
